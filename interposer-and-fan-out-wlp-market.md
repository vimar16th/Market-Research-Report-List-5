<p><p>Interposer and Fan-Out WLP are two types of advanced packaging technologies used in semiconductor manufacturing. Interposer technology involves placing an additional layer of silicon between the semiconductor die and the package substrate, allowing for higher performance and integration of different components. On the other hand, Fan-Out WLP technology involves redistributing the connections from the die to a larger area on the package substrate, enabling higher input/output counts and improved thermal performance. Both technologies are gaining popularity in the semiconductor industry for their ability to meet the increasing demand for smaller, faster, and more power-efficient electronic devices.</p></p>
