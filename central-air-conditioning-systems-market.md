<p><p>Central air conditioning systems can be categorized into two main types: Direct Expansion (DX) central air conditioning systems and Central Plant/Chilled Water Central Air Conditioning Systems. </p><p>DX systems use refrigerant as the cooling medium to cool the air directly in the air handling unit, while Central Plant/Chilled Water systems use a chiller plant to cool water, which is then circulated through coils in the air handling units to cool the air. </p><p>Both systems have their own advantages and disadvantages depending on factors such as cost, efficiency, and scalability.</p></p>
