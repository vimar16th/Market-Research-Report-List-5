<p><p>Hydraulic braking systems are classified into different types based on their mechanism of operation. The main types include hydraulic disc brakes, hydraulic drum brakes, and others. Hydraulic disc brakes use calipers to squeeze brake pads against a rotor to slow down the vehicle, offering precise and efficient braking performance. Hydraulic drum brakes, on the other hand, use brake shoes to press against the inner surface of a drum to generate friction and slow down the vehicle. Other types of hydraulic braking systems may include variations or combinations of these two main types.</p></p>
