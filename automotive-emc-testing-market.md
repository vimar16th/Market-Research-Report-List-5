<p><ul><li>Bulk Current Injection (BCI)</li><li>Conducted Emissions (CE)</li><li>Conducted Transient Emissions (CTE)</li><li>Conducted Transient Immunity (CTI)</li><li>Electrostatic Discharge (ESD)</li><li>Radiated Emissions (RE)</li><li>Radiated Immunity (RI)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Automotive EMC testing involves various types of testing methods to ensure that vehicles meet electromagnetic compatibility standards. These include Bulk Current Injection (BCI) for injecting current into cables, Conducted Emissions (CE) for measuring emissions through cables, Conducted Transient Emissions (CTE) for analyzing transient emissions, Conducted Transient Immunity (CTI) for testing immunity to transients, Electrostatic Discharge (ESD) for evaluating static electricity, Radiated Emissions (RE) for measuring emissions through radiation, and Radiated Immunity (RI) for testing immunity to radiation.</p></p>
