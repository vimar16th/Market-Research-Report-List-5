<p><p>Binocular biological microscopes are available in two main types - upright and inverted. </p><p>Upright microscopes have the objective lenses located above the stage, allowing for observation of samples placed on slides in a vertical position. These microscopes are commonly used in various fields like microbiology, botany, and cytology.</p><p>Inverted microscopes, on the other hand, have the objective lenses located below the stage, allowing for observation of samples in a horizontal position. These microscopes are commonly used in fields like cell biology, tissue culture, and embryology.</p></p>
