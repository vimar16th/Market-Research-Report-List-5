<p><p>Waste Heat Recovery Systems utilize the unused heat generated during industrial processes to produce electricity, thus increasing energy efficiency. Two main types of systems are the Steam Rankine Cycle and Organic Rankine Cycle. The Steam Rankine Cycle uses water and steam to generate power, while the Organic Rankine Cycle uses organic fluids such as refrigerants or oil. Both systems help to reduce energy costs and emissions by harnessing waste heat and converting it into valuable electricity.</p></p>
