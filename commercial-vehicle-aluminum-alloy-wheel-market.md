Casting involves pouring molten aluminum into a mold to create the wheel, resulting in a more cost-effective option suitable for mass production. Forging involves shaping the wheel by applying extreme pressure and heat to a solid block of aluminum, resulting in a stronger and more durable option. The "Other" market includes alternative manufacturing processes such as flow forming or hybrid construction techniques.</p></p>
